Learn

Keep Left

In New Zealand, all traffic travels on the left hand side of the road.

Overtaking

You may overtake to the right of a slow vehicle if it is safe and you have at least 100m of clear road at all times while performing the manoeuvre.
Always indicate at least 3 seconds prior to overtaking.

You can't overtake:

  • At a no-overtaking line marked by a yellow line on your side of the centreline

  • Near a pedestrian crossing, railway crossing or intersection

  • At a curve or crest of a hill where you do not have 100m of clear road during the entire manoeuvre and can't conduct the pass safely.

Speed Limits Apply on New Zealand Roads

Whatever speed you travel at ensure there is enough room between you and the vehicle in front so you can stop suddenly if required.


New Zealand speed limits are in kilometres per hour not miles.


Built up areas generally have speed limits of 50km/h however lower limits may apply.


"OPEN ROAD" limits are as follows

  • Cars, Motorcycles and Vans 100km/h
  • Heavy Vehicles 90km/h
  • Vehicle's towing caravans and/or trailers 90km/h

New Zealand Road Conditions

New Zealand roads are very different than the ones you may be used to.


They are generally hilly, narrow and have many twists and turns.


Although our roads are generally well-sealed there are still many gravel roads, slow down and drive to the conditions

Give yourself plenty of time to arrive at your destination and assume it will take longer than anticipated.

Gravel and Unsealed Roads

Avoid unsealed roads wherever possible.


If you must drive on them, remember they are generally narrow.

Drive slowly and when approaching oncoming traffic slow even further as dust can obscure your vision and stones can come up and chip or even crack your windscreen.

Motorways

Keep Left and always indicate at least three seconds before changing lanes.


The Maximum Speed Limit on New Zealand Motorways is 100km/h unless otherwise specified.


On a Motorway you can not

  • Walk or Cycle
  • Stop
  • Make a U Turn

Railway Crossings

Always give way to all rail traffic.

Some crossings are equipped with bells, sirens and barriers.
You must stop when these devices operate.
Signs are used at other crossings, you must not proceed unless
you are certain there is no rail traffic from either direction.
If the barrier arms are lowered or the sirens are sounding you must come to a complete stop until they stop ringing.

Slippery Roads

Slow down and drive with care on wet roads.

New Zealand roads are very unforgiving, and they are potentially dangerous when wet.
Slow down and drive with care to prevent you from losing control.

Drive to the Conditions on Icy Roads

Snow and Ice can make roads very dangerous, especially around mountain passes

Rental car companies will provide chains if you are likely to drive in these conditions.
Slow Down and ensure you follow the instructions of roadsigns, police and/or road workers.
Avoid nonessential travel wherever possible. To find out about the latest conditions please check the road status in the drive section.

Animals on the Road

If you come across farm animals on the road, slow down and do not sound your horn

You may have to stop to let them go past, slowly approach behind them and follow the farmer's instructions.

Traffic Lights

At traffic lights the following applies:

  • Red means stop
  • Yellow means stop if it safe to do so
  • Green means proceed if it is safe

  • Red Arrow means stop and don't turn
  • Yellow means stop if it safe to do so
  • Green means turn if it is safe
You must signal at least 3 seconds before turning

One Lane Bridges

Stop before approaching the bridge to ensure it is clear.

The circular sign with the red arrow means you have to give way to all oncoming traffic.

The rectangular sign with the large arrow in your direction means you have the right of way.
If a vehicle is on the bridge or can't stop you must give way.

Roundabouts

Give way to all traffic to your right

Enter the roundabout to the left

Signal at a roundabout if you plan to turn and when you pass the exit before the one you plan to take.

Parking Restrictions

Do not park on a broken yellow line
Do not park within 6 metres of an intersection or pedestrian crossing
Do not park facing oncoming traffic


Do not stop or park in the area marked by No Stopping Signs.

Do not stop or park in the area marked by No Stopping Signs during the time specified.

  You may park for up to 60 minutes in the location indicated by the sign.
  The number on the sign represents the number of minutes you are allowed to park in that location for.

Pedestrian Crossings

Give way to pedestrians on both sides of the crossing

Slow Down when approaching and be prepared to stop

Do not overtake near a crossing.

Intersections

Look in all directions, obey road signs and traffic lights.
If road signs don't determine who has right of way follow these rules:

  • If you are turning give way to vehicles going straight
  • When turning right give way to vehicles on your turning left
  • At a T intersection and driveways the side road must give way to traffic on the straight road
  • Otherwise give way to vehicles coming from your right.

Keep Left Unless Passing

When a dual lane road is available use the left lane unless overtaking.

In passing bays, slow vehicle bays and passing lanes always use the left-most lane and allow others to pass.
Allow vehicles to pass you as soon as it is safe and practical to do so.

Important Information

Drivers Licence:

You must have a current Licence and/or an international licence

The minimum age for hiring a rental car is generally 21


Impaired Driving:

Drugs and/or medication which can effect your driving is illegal

There is a zero alcohol limit for drivers under 20;
drivers over 20 are advised to consumer no more than two standard drinks before driving


Seatbelts:

You must wear seatbelts, failing to do so can incur a $150 fine


Cellphones:

You must not use a mobile phone while driving unless it is completely hands free
or mounted to the vehicle and touched infrequently.


Crashes:

If you are involved in a crash you must stop and call the police (111) if someone is injured.
Obtain vehicle registration numbers and notify the insurance company and/or rental car company within 24 hours.
If no one is hurt give your name and address to the property owner or the police if the owner cant be located.
